Analysis Overview

Airplane crash in Aviation Industry is a major concern due to many fatalities in a crash. Even though aircraft crash is much lesser in frequency than other transport form of medium, it still faces a major concern both among people and the industry persons. The possible cause for an aircraft accident is infinite. It can be due to improper communication, technology failure, operating issues and so on. Narrowing down the possibility of crash occurrence is a prominent element to focus more on high probable causes of the aircraft crash.

Some of the major factors for the aircraft crash are:

Pilot Error

Pilot error is the most common cause of aviation accidents. A slight error or delay in handling critical tasks correctly could result in a disaster.

Weather Conditions

weather is the cause of 12% of aviation accidents.Lightning strikes are a particularly dangerous hazard for airplanes.

Mechanical Error

Mechanical errors are the second leading cause of aviation accidents, accounting for 22% of all crashes. Mechanical errors could occur because of a flaw inherent in the aircraft's design or because a mechanical part was not properly installed.

Air Traffic Controller Error

Air traffic controllers have one of the most stressful jobs in the world. Any misstep or failure to follow proper air traffic control procedures can lead to a fatal plane crash.

Objectives of the Analysis

The prominent and most essential element of the crash will be analysed to understand more about the crash occurrence and it will help in assessing the underlying problem for the crash of the aircrafts.

Type of aircraft which is involved in high number of crashes

The aircraft type which is more prone to the crash occurrence is one of the primary objective of the analysis

Route in which crash occurred

The route where accidents occurred most often will shed light on the factors contributing to the flight crashes

Country/Region in which aircraft crash happens most

Dtermining the region of crash occurrence is also a prominent factor of consideration that will help in explaining the regional factors attributing to crash

Flight Operators who involved in Crashes

The inefficiency of the flight operator factor can be be analysed by this and the operators with more crashes will provide an overview about the operating characteristics of various flight operators.

Analysis

The analysis of the data by segregation of various factors can be done to evaluate the prominent factors and its corresponding reasons for the flight crash occurrence. The visualization of the crash patterns in the form of graphs will help in easier and quicker identification of the underlying patterns in the aircraft accident pattern.



The data visualization is a most effective approach in examining the old data since it provides graphical representation of the data and provides more information in a timely manner. Therefore, the analysis of the dataset can be started with the visualization of the data that are available in hand and further analysis approaches can be inferred from the analysis of the data which are being visualized.

Crash Analysis by Type of Aircraft

The graphical output of the data exhibits that aircraft model “Douglas DC-3” involves in higher number of crashed in relative to other types of aircrafts which is followed by DHC-6 Twin Otter 300 and Douglas C-47. The aircraft Douglas DC-3 involved in more than 300 crashes and it is comparatively higher than other type of aircrafts. Therefore, it provides an interesting viewpoint for this aircraft since it involves in many accidents.

Crash Analysis by Route

The graph shows that the crash occurs more predominantly during training of the aircraft. It accounts for about 80 crashes of the flight. After that, the most crash is followed by Sightseeing, Test flight and Testing of the aircraft. The commercial flight routes saw lesser crashes based on the inference of the graph. It means that the most crashes were occurred due to military type of aircraft and it is also during the training stage.

Crash Analysis by Country/Region

The output shows that the large number of accidents happens at Brazil which is followed by Alaska and Russia. These three regions alone result in more than 175 accidents each. The probable reason for the accidents could be due to various reasons but the information obtained above provides more insight into the area in which one has to look to explore more reasons for the cause of accident.

Analysis by Flight Operators

The flights which are being operated Aeroflot, a Russian Airlines, invovled in more number of crashes with the Boeing Aircrafts. It is followed by US Air Force and Air France. The possible reasons for the more crashes by the Russian airline can be analysed further to uncover more related factors.
